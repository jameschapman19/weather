Reading the Forecasts

Two complementary approaches — use them together for the clearest picture

🎲

Ensemble Forecasts

Best for: understanding uncertainty & the range of possible outcomes

  • Run the same model 30–50 times with slightly different starting conditions
  • The shaded band (25–75th percentile) shows the most likely 50% of outcomes
  • A narrow band means high confidence; a wide band means genuine uncertainty
  • The median line is the "middle of the pack" — not a single best-guess run
  • Multiple models agreeing narrows the band further and boosts confidence
Use to answer: "How confident can we be in this forecast?"
🎯

Operational (Deterministic) Forecasts

Best for: the single most-likely scenario at higher resolution

  • Each centre runs one high-resolution model — their best-guess forecast
  • Typically higher spatial resolution than ensemble runs (captures local terrain)
  • No spread information — you see the predicted value, not the uncertainty around it
  • When multiple centres agree, confidence is high; when they diverge, there's real uncertainty
  • More useful closer to the event (<5 days) when uncertainty naturally reduces
Use to answer: "What exactly does each centre predict will happen?"
